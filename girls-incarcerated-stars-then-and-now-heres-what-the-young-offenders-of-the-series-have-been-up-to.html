    Standout stars of the docuseries 'Girls Incarcerated' after 5 years

    Netflix released the docuseries 'Girls Incarcerated: Young and Locked Up' in 2018, offering an unfiltered glance into the lives of young female offenders within the criminal justice system. Wondering where the cast is today? Read on.

    Brianna Guerra

    Also Read: When will 'The Trust: A Game of Greed' air? Release date, plot, and cast of Netflix game show

    At 17, an outspoken Brianna Guerra faced juvenile detention for offenses like armed robbery and drug use. Despite a probation violation after release, she now thrives in Indianapolis, managing a successful YouTube channel with 130,000 followers. A loving mom to sons Cartier and Corleone, Brianna cherishes every aspect of motherhood, expressing profound love for her children.

    Paige Mcatee

    In the juvenile detention center, Paige Mcatee battled anger and attitude issues. Sharing her mom's prison history, she unexpectedly befriended nemesis Heidi Lankin. A mom of three today, Paige juggles roles as a lash and nail technician, pursuing a future as a sonographer through her ongoing studies.

    Heidi Lakin

    Every teen on the series bears a powerful tale, but Heidi Lakin's was particularly distressing. She disclosed childhood sexual abuse, leading to profound anger issues. Her release faced delays due to behavioral concerns. Currently free, Heidi shared the heartbreaking news of a recent miscarriage with followers, captioning a graphic photo with "RIP, little one."

    Aubrey Wilson 

    Aubrey Wilson was imprisoned for nine months after completing the center's substance abuse program. However, she fled mere weeks after her release to deal with her troubled connection with her mother, who also struggled with drug addiction. She remained active on social media in the years following the show, posting a lot of selfies. Aubrey died on September 30, 2022, following a long struggle with drug addiction, her family confirmed to In Touch in October.

    Taryn Twine

    The high school gymnast's tale was heart-wrenching, as Taryn requested juvenile corrections as penance for a fatal car accident involving her best friend. Recognized for her swift burgundy shirt achievement and a passion for cartwheels, she recently celebrated the birth of a baby girl in early 2022, as per her Facebook page.

    Sarah Maxwell

    Sarah had been navigating foster care since age 5 due to her mother's drug abuse. Her teenage troubles stemmed from a desire to retaliate against her mom. Presently thriving, Sarah expresses gratitude for the overwhelming support she received after the show's premiere, acknowledging it on Facebook with a heartfelt "thank you" to all. 

    Najwa Pollard

    Najwa bravely shared her tumultuous childhood, marked by fatherly abuse, and the heartbreaking reality of being indefinitely confined to the Madison Juvenile Correctional Facility due to a lack of family release options. However, Najwa seems to have gained her freedom since, as evidenced by her Instagram activity and the celebration of her 21st birthday in June 2022.

    Armani Buckner

    Armani and Alexis Miller's relationship captivated viewers, but it didn't end well. Armani later dated Parris, expressing lasting love on Facebook. However, a recent update indicates a shift, as her page now reads "single AF."
