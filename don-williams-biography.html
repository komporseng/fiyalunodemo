    Who was Don Williams?

    Don Williams was an American singer, and songwriter(May 27, 1939-September 8, 2017). He was mostly known as the 2010 inductee to the Country Music Hall of Fame.

    Williams had sung popular ballads and one country hits. He was a member of the folk-pop group Pozo-Seco Singers. He was also nominated for the Grammy award in 2013.

    Don Williams: Professional life, Career

    Don Williams gave a start to his career when he formed a band named the first band The Strangers Two with Lofton Kline. In 1964, Susan Taylor convinced him to be a part of the band named Pozo-Seco Singers.

    They recorded their single debut ‘Time in 1966′ when they were signed to Edmark Records. In the same year, they released another debut album Time. They released their three albums when they were signed by Columbia Records.

    The band Pozo-Seco Singers disbanded in 1971, then he started his solo career. He joined Jack Music Inc where he was a songwriter.

    In June 1973, his first solo album was Don Williams Volume One which was released by JMI records. He released several records in the 1970s.

    On 25th September 1974, Don Williams released his third album called  Don Williams Vol. 3, which was released by ABC/Dot Records.

    In 1976, Don Williams released his fifth album called Harmony. In the same year, he was nominated for the Academy of Country Music. He was also nominated for ACM Top Male Vocalist.

    For the next three years, Don Williams was nominated for the same award. Finally, in 1978, he won the ACM Single Record of the Year – Tulsa Time.

    In 1978, he was honored by the Country Music Association. He was also regarded as the CMA Male Vocalist of the Year.

    In 2006, he announced his retirement from music in 2016, but later he was also nominated for the Grammy award in 2013.
