    Career

    • Pauline joined politics in 1994 and served as an Ipswich City Councillor until 1995, when administrative changes necessitated early elections. She was defeated in her re-election bid.
    • She joined the Liberal Party of Australia in 1996 and ran as the Liberal candidate for the House of Representatives from Oxley in the federal elections that year. The party quickly disendorsed her, and after winning, she ran for parliament as an independent candidate.
    • In her inaugural address, she emphasized the themes of race and immigration. She believed that Australia’s lax immigration policies resulted in fewer work opportunities for its citizens. She said other ethnicities and cultures are encroaching on Australia while providing nothing in return.
    • In 2003, she was defeated by John Tingle of the Shooters Party in the New South Wales (NSW) Upper House election.
      In the 2004 Federal Elections, she ran as an independent candidate and garnered just 31.77 percent of the needed quota of primary votes. She was able to recoup her $150,000 deposit from the Australian Electoral Commission.
    • On May 24, 2007, she established the ‘Pauline’s United Australia Party’ (PUAP). She ran as an independent candidate from Beaudesert in the 2009 Queensland state election, finishing third.
    • In February 2010, she declared that she would de-register PUAP and relocate to the United Kingdom. In November 2014, she was elected as the head of One Nation. However, after rejoining the One Nation party, she ran in the 2013 federal elections for a Senate seat from New South Wales and was again defeated.
